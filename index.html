Yahtzee!
Welcome to Yahtzee! In this game, your goal is to maximize the amount of points you score. Each turn, you make an initial roll first, then choose dice to keep. You then take the dice you decided to not keep and roll them again. You then choose what dice, out of all of the five, to keep. You finally roll one more time, with the dice being your final ones for that turn. For scoring, if you score a number, your score will be the total of just the number you choose. For example, if you have 4 5s in your dice, and you score your 5s, your score will go up by 20. For the other scoring meathods, for both 3 of a kind and 4 of a kind (having 3 or 4 of the same dice value respectively) your score increases by the total of all of the dice. For full house (having two of one number and three of another), your score increases by 25, regardless of the values of your dice. For small straight (having four dice with consecutive values), your score increases by 30. For a large straight (having five dice with consecutive values), your score increases by 40. Next, for chance (there is no rule to score this), you add up the total value of all of the dice. Finally, for a Yahtzee, for the first one, you get 50 points, and for every one after this, you get 100 points. This is the only one you can score more than once. Every other score can only be used once. Also, if you can not play any of the scores, you choose one of them, and will recieve no points. this counts as your use of that score. Each player gets 12 turns, which are shown at the bottom of the page.
